Ecumenical Women's Retreat

Drongen Abbey

1-2 October 2022

Finding Joy

The theme for the weekend is 'Those who sow in tears... will reap with songs of Joy'. In this retreat we will be taking time together to creatively reflect with God and with each other on where we find ourselves now in the light of the global pandemic, the war in Ukraine, climate change, Brexit and the other crises affecting the world as we know it.

Our speaker is Rhona Knight who comes to us as a healthcare professional and an ordained priest, with years of experience in working through trauma and promoting wellbeing. We will be helped to reorientate ourselves toward the God who holds the greater story - the story of justice, of grace, of mercy and of hope.

How to get here

The retreat is hosted at Drongen Abbey, Drongenplein 26/27, 9031 Gent.

By train

The Abbey is situated less than 10 minutes walk from the Drongen (Tronchiennes) train station, just outside of Gent-Sint-Pieters.

There are trains which stop in Drongen (Tronchiennes) at 10:49 and 11:49.

By bus

Buses also run from Gent-Sint-Pieters station to Drongen, stopping very near the Abbey.

Our Speaker

Revd Dr Rhona Knight

Rhona was a GP ethicist, academic and medical educator for 25 years. She was ordained in the Church of England in 2016 and led on teaching pastoral care and ethics at Lincoln School of Theology.

Now retired, she continues to research, write and teach particularly in the area of boundaries and trauma in ministry. She is a retreat leader, spiritual director and pastoral supervisor. She is married to Chris and they have three adult sons and a growing number of daughters-in-law.

About

Welcome to Belgium Women Together (formerly Brussels Women Together) events page. BWT is a group that seeks to draw Christian women together from English speaking churches for ecumenical retreats.

This annual retreat weekend is an opportunity to take time out together for rest, relaxation, and reflection on an aspect of our faith. Whether you are looking for an oasis to recharge your body and spirit or the chance to forge and deepen friendships, the weekend away is for you!

Back to top

This retreat is organised annually by women from a variety of churches/denominations in Belgium. This year most of the organisers attend All Saints' Waterloo, St Martha and St Mary's Leuven, and Holy Trinity Brussels.
